SEPA SEASON 5 TOURNAMENT RULES AND REGULATIONS

Organizing a football tournament requires a clear set of rules and regulations to ensure fairness, safety, and enjoyment for all participants. Here’s a general outline of rules and regulations commonly used in SEPA SEASON 5 football tournaments:

1. General Rules

  • Tournament Format: This tournament will begin with a group stage followed by a knockout stage.
  • Team Composition: Each team can register up to 22 players, with 11 players on the field, including a goalkeeper. Up to 4 substitutes are allowed per match.
  • Match Duration: Standard match duration is 90 minutes, split into two halves of 45 minutes each, with a 15-minute halftime break. Adjustments may be made based on time constraints.
  • Point System (for round-robin/league formats):
    • Win: 3 points
    • Draw: 1 point
    • Loss: 0 points
  • Tie-Breaker Rules: In case of a tie in points, criteria such as goal difference, head-to-head results, goals scored, or a penalty shootout can be used to determine the winner.

2. Player Eligibility

  • Age Groups: All adult age categories are allowed.
  • Player Registration: Teams must submit a list of registered players before the tournament begins. Only registered players are eligible to participate.
  • Identity Verification: Players may need to present their license identification to verify their identity.
  • Kickoff Time: Matches are scheduled to kick off at 4 PM.

3. Match Regulations

  • Substitutions: A maximum of 4 substitutions is allowed per match.
  • Offside Rule: Standard FIFA offside rules apply unless specified otherwise.
  • Fouls and Misconduct: Standard FIFA rules regarding fouls, yellow cards, and red cards apply. Accumulation of yellow cards may result in suspension with a fine of ₦500 for a yellow card and ₦1000 for a red card.
  • Penalty Shootouts: If a match is tied in the knockout rounds and a winner must be decided, a penalty shootout may be used.
  • Extra Time: No extra time will be played in the knockout rounds; matches will go directly to a penalty shootout if tied.

4. Equipment and Attire

  • Uniforms: Teams must wear matching uniforms with numbered jerseys. Goalkeepers must wear a color that distinguishes them from outfield players and referees.
  • Footwear: Only appropriate football boots (e.g., studs) are allowed. No metal studs unless specified.
  • Shin Guards: Mandatory for all players.
  • Ball Specification: The tournament will use size 5 footballs for all matches.

5. Conduct and Discipline

  • Code of Conduct: All players, coaches, and spectators must adhere to a code of conduct that promotes respect and sportsmanship.
  • Disciplinary Actions: Penalties for misconduct, such as verbal abuse, unsporting behavior, or violent conduct, may include match bans or fines.
  • Protests and Appeals: The FA will be present at all matches to investigate any appeals.

6. Referees and Officials

  • Appointment of Officials: Referees, assistant referees, and other officials are appointed by the organizing committee.
  • Authority: Referees have full authority to enforce the laws of the game during the match.
  • Dispute Resolution: Any disputes or decisions related to the match are resolved by the referee, with the possibility of an appeal to the organizing committee.

7. Health and Safety

  • Medical Facilities: On-site medical facilities or personnel will be available during all matches.
  • Weather Conditions: Matches may be postponed or abandoned due to severe weather conditions.
  • Player Safety: Guidelines will be in place for dealing with injuries, including concussion protocols and the role of medical personnel.

8. Logistics

  • Match Scheduling: A detailed schedule of matches, including dates, times, and venues, will be provided.
  • Venue Rules: Specify rules for the venue, such as access points, parking, and areas restricted to players and officials.
  • Awards and Prizes: Prizes include ₦1,000,000 for 1st place, ₦500,000 for 2nd place, ₦300,000 for 3rd place, and ₦200,000 for 4th place, along with trophies for all winners.

9. Financial Regulations

  • Entry Fees: The entry fee for each team is ₦40,000.
  • Prize Money: The total prize money distribution is ₦2,000,000.
  • Sponsorships: The tournament is sponsored by Hon. Chairman Suleja LG, Hon. Isiyaku Bawa Naibi (IBN).

10. Miscellaneous

  • Disclaimers: The organizing committee is not liable for any injuries or damages during the tournament.
  • Modifications: The organizing committee reserves the right to modify rules and regulations as necessary, with appropriate notice to teams.

These rules should be enforced consistently throughout the event.
